# FORGE Conditioning Library V1

> Universal conditioning ontology reverse-engineered from BCCI, ECB, Cricket Australia, ASCA, NSCA, Rugby S&C, Soccer S&C, Tennis S&C, Badminton S&C, and Athletics Sprint coaches.
> No sport-specific systems. Universal energy system taxonomy + methods + protocols.
> Conditioning prescriptions can be generated from this document alone.

---

## SECTION 1: Energy System Taxonomy

### 10 Universal Energy Systems

| # | System | Primary Adaptation | Intensity | Duration | Work:Rest | Sport Relevance |
|---|--------|-------------------|-----------|----------|-----------|----------------|
| 1 | **Aerobic Capacity** | Mitochondrial density, capillary network, fat oxidation | RPE 3-4, Zone 2, 60-70% HRmax | 20-60 min continuous | No rest (continuous) | Foundation for all sports. Recovery between plays. |
| 2 | **Aerobic Power** | VO2 max, lactate clearance, stroke volume | RPE 6-7, Zone 4, 85-90% HRmax | 2-8 min work bouts | 1:1 to 2:1 work:rest | Field sports, court sports, high-volume positions. |
| 3 | **Extensive Tempo** | Work capacity, movement efficiency, aerobic support for speed | RPE 5-6, 70-80% max speed | 10-60s work bouts | 1:1 to 1:3 work:rest | Pre-season base, return to play, volume accumulation. |
| 4 | **Intensive Tempo** | Lactate threshold, fatigue resistance at higher speeds | RPE 7-8, 80-90% max speed | 30-120s work bouts | 1:2 to 1:4 work:rest | Late pre-season, sport-specific conditioning. |
| 5 | **Repeated Sprint Ability** | Repeated high-intensity output, phosphocreatine recovery | RPE 8-9, maximal sprint | 3-10s work bouts | 1:3 to 1:8 work:rest | Field sports, court sports. The primary field sport conditioning quality. |
| 6 | **Speed Endurance** | ATP-PC recovery, glycolytic capacity, max velocity maintenance | RPE 9-10, near-maximal to maximal | 6-60s work bouts | 1:5 to 1:15 work:rest | All sports requiring repeated maximal efforts. |
| 7 | **Alactic Speed** | Maximal velocity, acceleration mechanics, neural drive | RPE 10, maximal | 1-6s work bouts | 1:10+ work:rest | All sports. Speed is trained fresh or not at all. |
| 8 | **Lactate Tolerance** | Buffering capacity, ability to perform under high acidity | RPE 9-10, maximal effort | 30-120s work bouts | 1:2 to 1:5 work:rest | High-intensity intermittent sports, middle-distance events. |
| 9 | **Power Maintenance** | Maintain explosive output under fatigue | RPE 8-9, maximal intent | 3-10s work bouts | 1:5 to 1:15 work:rest | In-season, peaking phase, competition period. |
| 10 | **Recovery Conditioning** | Active recovery, blood flow, movement quality | RPE 2-3, Zone 1-2, 50-60% HRmax | 10-30 min | No rest (continuous) | Day after competition, deload weeks, return from illness. |

## SECTION 2: Method Library

### Aerobic Capacity Methods

| Method | Description | Typical Athlete |
|--------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Long Slow Distance** | Continuous running at conversational pace. 20-60 min. | All athletes in off-season base phase. |
| **Recovery Jog** | Very low intensity jogging. 10-20 min. Between efforts or active recovery days. | All athletes in-season, post-competition. |
| **Walking Circuit** | Interval walking with mobility. 20-30 min. | Return-to-sport, deload, beginner. |
| **Cross-Training Aerobic** | Cycling, swimming, elliptical at low intensity. 20-40 min. | Alternative for high-impact athletes, injury modification. |
| **Extended Tempo Walk** | Brisk walking (6-7 km/h) with uphill sections. 30-45 min. | Pre-season base building, low-impact alternative. |

### Aerobic Power Methods

| Method | Description | Typical Athlete |
|--------|-------------|-----------------|
| **MAS Running** | Maximal aerobic speed intervals. Run at MAS pace (distance completed in 60s of all-out effort). | Off-season and pre-season team sport. BCCI standard. |
| **Threshold Runs** | Sustained effort at lactate threshold pace (80-85% HRmax). 15-30 min continuous or broken. | Advanced off-season, endurance sport. |
| **Cruise Intervals** | Sub-maximal intervals at threshold pace with short recovery. | Pre-season, intermediate to advanced. |
| **Yo-Yo Intermittent Recovery Test** | 2x20m shuttles at increasing speed with 10s active recovery. Standardised test and training protocol. | Field sport pre-season and in-season monitoring. |
| **30-20 Intermittent Fitness Test** | 30s shuttle runs at increasing speed with 20s passive recovery. | Court sports, field sports. |
| **Treadmill Intervals** | Controlled pace intervals on treadmill. 1-4 min work bouts at 85-90% HRmax with equal or shorter rest. | Off-season, indoor training, controlled environment. |

### Extensive Tempo Methods

| Method | Description | Typical Athlete |
|--------|-------------|-----------------|
| **60m Tempo** | 60m runs at 70-80% max speed, walk-back recovery. 10-15 reps. The BCCI standard. | Cricket, field sports, pre-season base. |
| **100m Tempo** | 100m runs at 70-80% max speed, walk-back recovery. 8-12 reps. | Track athletes, speed development blocks. |
| **Shuttle Tempo** | Multi-directional shuttles at sub-maximal intensity. 20-30s work. | Court sports, multi-directional field sports. |
| **Stride-throughs** | 80-100m accelerations building from jog to 80% speed. Walk-back recovery. | Warm-up progression, speed-day primer. |
| **Broken Tempo** | Multiple shorter runs with brief rest adding up to a continuous volume target. | High-volume pre-season blocks. |

### Intensive Tempo Methods

| Method | Description | Typical Athlete |
|--------|-------------|-----------------|
| **200m Repeats** | 200m at 80-90% max speed. 2-3 min rest. 5-8 reps. | Soccer, rugby, cricket late pre-season. |
| **300m Repeats** | 300m at 80-85% max speed. 3-4 min rest. 4-6 reps. | Advanced pre-season, middle-distance. |
| **400m Repeats** | 400m at 75-85% max speed. 3-5 min rest. 4-6 reps. | Cricket (classic BCCI/ECB pre-season protocol). |
| **Descending Ladder** | Descending distances (800 → 400 → 200 → 100) at increasing intensity. Full recovery between. | Advanced athletes, multi-sport, peaking. |
| **Pyramid** | Ascending then descending distances. Building intensity as distance drops. | Cricket, rugby, advanced team sport. |

### Repeated Sprint Ability Methods

| Method | Description | Typical Athlete |
|--------|-------------|-----------------|
| **30m RSA** | 30m maximal sprint every 30s. Continue for 5-10 min. BCCI standard. | Cricket, field sports, in-season monitoring. |
| **40m RSA** | 40m maximal sprint with shortened recovery. 6-10 reps. | Rugby, soccer. |
| **Multi-Shuttle RSA** | 4-6 x 20-30m shuttles per set. Multiple sets with full recovery between sets. | Court sports, badminton, tennis. |
| **Game-Specific RSA** | Sport-specific distances and directions at maximal intensity with sport-specific work:rest ratios. | All sports in-season maintenance. |
| **RSA Cluster** | 3 x 30m sprints with 20s rest between sprints. 1 cluster = 3 sprints. 3-4 clusters with 4 min rest between clusters. | Advanced field sport, pre-season. |
| **30m Fatigue Maintenance** | 30m sprint start every 20th second for 6 reps. Power maintenance under fatigue. | Cricket, speed-endurance focus. |

### Speed Endurance Methods

| Method | Description | Typical Athlete |
|--------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Broken 400s** | 400m broken into segments (e.g., 200m + 100m + 100m) with very short rest. Hold pace across all segments. | 400m runners, advanced team sport. |
| **150m Repeats** | 150m at near-maximal speed. Full recovery (3-5 min). 4-6 reps. | Sprinters, field sport speed block. |
| **Flying 30s** | 30-40m max speed from a flying start (20-30m build-up). Full recovery. | Advanced sprinters, rugby, soccer speed specialists. |
| **Cut-downs** | Start at a moderate pace, finish at max speed. E.g., 150m: first 50m at 70%, second 50m at 85%, final 50m at 100%. | Speed development, advanced athletes. |
| **Speed Maintenance Sets** | 80m at 95-100% with walk-back recovery. 4-6 reps per set. Multiple sets. | In-season speed maintenance, peaking phase. |

### Alactic Speed Methods

| Method | Description | Typical Athlete |
|--------|-------------|-----------------|
| **10-30m Acceleration** | Maximal acceleration from various starts (3-point, 2-point, crouch, rolling). Full recovery (2-3 min). | All athletes. Speed development. |
| **Flying Sprints** | 20-30m max velocity from a flying start. Full recovery. 4-6 reps. | Advanced speed block. |
| **Resisted Sprints** | Sled or band-resisted acceleration sprints. 10-20m. Full recovery. 4-6 reps. | Strength-power athletes, acceleration focus. |
| **Contrast Sprints** | Resisted sprint (10m) immediately followed by unresisted sprint (20m). Full recovery. 3-5 pairs. | Advanced speed block, PAP potentiation. |
| **Flying 10m Timed** | 10m max velocity segment after 20-30m build-up. Timed. 4-6 reps. | Sprinters, advanced speed monitoring. |

### Lactate Tolerance Methods

| Method | Description | Typical Athlete |
|--------|-------------|-----------------|
| **400m Repeats (hard)** | 400m at 90-95% max effort. 3-5 min rest. 4-6 reps. | 400/800m runners, advanced team sport pre-season. |
| **300m Repeats (hard)** | 300m at 95% max effort. 3-4 min rest. 4-6 reps. | Middle-distance, advanced team sport. |
| **Broken 800s** | 800m broken into 400m + 200m + 200m with 30-60s rest. Hold pace across all segments. | 800m runners, advanced conditioning. |
| **Short-Long-Short** | 200m (fast) → 400m (fast) → 200m (fast). 4-5 min rest between. Full recovery between sets. | Advanced multi-sport, peaking. |
| **Sprint Float Sprint** | 100m sprint → 100m float (70%) → 100m sprint. Walk recovery. Repeat 3-4 times. | Sprinters, speed endurance. |

### Power Maintenance Methods

| Method | Description | Typical Athlete |
|--------|-------------|-----------------|
| **30m Fatigue Test** | 30m sprint every 20th second for 6 reps. Measure fatigue decrement. <5% decrement = good power maintenance. | Cricket, field sports, in-season monitoring. |
| **Jump-Sprint Complex** | CMJ → 10m sprint. Maximal intent on both. 30-60s rest between reps. 4-6 reps. | Power maintenance, peaking phase. |
| **Repeated Hops** | 5 repeated broad jumps for distance. 1 min rest. 4-6 reps. Track distance decrement. | Lower body power maintenance. |
| **Med Ball Power Endurance** | Med ball overhead throw → med ball chest pass → med ball rotational throw. Complex of 3 throws, minimal rest between throws, 2 min between complexes. 4-6 complexes. | Upper body power maintenance. |

### Recovery Conditioning Methods

| Method | Description | Typical Athlete |
|--------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Active Recovery Jog** | 10-20 min at 50-60% HRmax. Self-selected pace. | Day after competition, deload week. |
| **Recovery Walk** | 20-30 min brisk walk. | Low-impact recovery option. |
| **Mobility Circuit** | Full body mobility sequence. 10-15 min. | Deload, return-to-sport, warm-up replacement on recovery days. |
| **Aqua Jogging** | Deep water running. 15-25 min at low intensity. | High-impact sport recovery, injury return. |
| **Cycling Recovery** | Stationary bike at low resistance. 15-20 min. RPE 2-3. | Alternative for lower body impact reduction. |

### 3.2 Aerobic Power Protocols

#### AP-001: MAS 60m Standard

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Name** | MAS 60m Standard |
| **Objective** | Develop maximal aerobic speed. Run at individual MAS pace. A BCCI and ASCA standard protocol. |
| **System** | Aerobic Power |
| **Level** | Intermediate to Elite |
| **Environment** | Track, field (measured 60m distance) |
| **Duration** | 18-24 min |
| **Work** | 60m in target time (determined by MAS test: distance covered in 60s all-out = MAS pace per 60m). Typically 8-10s per 60m. |
| **Rest** | 27s passive rest (1:3 to 1:4 work:rest ratio) |
| **Sets** | 6 reps × 3 sets. 5 min rest between sets. |
| **Total Volume** | 18-30 reps. 1,080-1,800m total. |
| **Fatigue Score** | 3/5 |
| **Progression** | Reduce rest (27s → 25s → 20s). Increase reps per set (6 → 8). Decrease set rest (5 → 4 min). |
| **Regression** | Increase rest (27s → 35s). Reduce reps (6 → 4 per set). Reduce sets (3 → 2). Reduce distance (60m → 50m). |

## SECTION 5: FORGE Conditioning Rules

### 5.1 Conditioning Placement Rules

**CR-001:** Conditioning is always the final training block in any session. Never before power, never before strength, never in the middle of a session.

**CR-002:** When conditioning is combined with gym work in the same session, the session order is: Warm-up → Power → Strength → Accessory/Core → Conditioning → Cool-down.

**CR-003:** A session may have conditioning OR speed work, never both as primary stimuli. If both appear, speed is the primary and conditioning is low-intensity recovery (RPE <4).

**CR-004:** Conditioning sessions on speed days must be aerobic capacity (Zone 2) only. Never intensive tempo or RSA on speed days.

### 5.2 Conditioning After Strength Rules

**CR-005:** Conditioning after strength is acceptable ONLY if the strength stimulus was the primary goal and conditioning is supplementary.

**CR-006:** After heavy strength (RPE 8-10 on primary lifts), conditioning must be aerobic capacity (Zone 2, RPE 3-4) or very low volume RSA. Do not program intensive tempo or lactate tolerance after heavy strength.

**CR-007:** After moderate strength (RPE 5-7), conditioning may be extensive tempo or moderate aerobic power work.

**CR-008:** If the strength session was lower body dominant, conditioning should involve lower body work (running). If the strength session was upper body dominant, conditioning may involve lower body or full body work.

**CR-009:** Do not program conditioning on back-to-back heavy strength days. At least one day between heavy strength + conditioning sessions for CNS recovery.

### 5.3 Power Maintenance Rules

**CR-010:** Power maintenance (RSA, short sprints, jumps) is the FIRST priority in any speed-power session. Conditioning is secondary.

**CR-011:** In-season: maintain power with 1 session/week of RSA or speed endurance at low volume (4-6 max efforts). Do not let conditioning volume crowd out power preservation.

**CR-012:** Power maintenance sessions must have full recovery between efforts (minimum 1:5 work:rest for RSA, >1:10 for alactic speed).

**CR-013:** Measure power maintenance quality with the 30m fatigue test. If decrement exceeds 10% from rep 1 to rep 6, reduce conditioning volume and increase recovery.

### 5.4 Sprint Freshness Rules

**CR-014:** Sprint work (alactic speed) ALWAYS requires fresh CNS. No conditioning before sprinting. No strength before sprinting. No extensive tempo before sprinting.

**CR-015:** On sprint days, the session order is: Warm-up → Sprint mechanics → Acceleration → Max velocity → (optional: light plyometric) → Cool-down. Conditioning is absent or very low intensity.

**CR-016:** Sprint sessions must have a minimum of 48 hours between them for CNS recovery. High-volume sprint sessions require 72 hours.

**CR-017:** Do not program resisted sprinting (sled, bands) within 48 hours of a heavy lower body strength session.

### 5.5 Weekly Frequency Rules

**CR-018:** Off-season conditioning frequency: 2-3 sessions/week maximum. More than 3 conditioning sessions/week interferes with strength and power development.

**CR-019:** Pre-season conditioning frequency: 2-3 sessions/week. Shift from aerobic capacity toward RSA and intensive tempo.

**CR-020:** In-season conditioning frequency: 1-2 sessions/week. Maintenance only. Sport practice provides the majority of conditioning stimulus.

**CR-021:** Never program more than 2 high-intensity conditioning sessions (RPE 8+) in a single week. One per week is sufficient for most athletes.

**CR-022:** Recovery conditioning (Zone 2, RPE 2-3) can be performed daily if needed. It does not count toward the high-intensity limit.

**CR-023:** One day per week must have zero conditioning stimulus. Complete recovery day or skill-only day.

### 5.6 Deload Conditioning Rules

**CR-024:** During deload weeks, conditioning volume drops by 50-60% of normal weekly volume.

**CR-025:** During deload weeks, conditioning intensity caps at RPE 6. No maximal efforts. No RSA. No lactate tolerance.

**CR-026:** During deload weeks, conditioning shifts to aerobic capacity (Zone 2) and recovery modalities.

**CR-027:** After a competition, conditioning is limited to recovery jogging or walking for 48 hours minimum before resuming structured conditioning.

### 5.7 Energy System Integration Rules

**CR-028:** Within a training week, schedule energy systems from lowest neurological demand to highest: Aerobic capacity → Extensive tempo → Aerobic power → Intensive tempo → RSA → Speed endurance → Alactic speed.

**CR-029:** Do not program alactic speed and lactate tolerance in the same week. They train opposing qualities (freshness vs. fatigue tolerance).

**CR-030:** RSA and speed endurance may coexist in the same week if separated by 48+ hours and not in the same session.

**CR-031:** Aerobic capacity and alactic speed may coexist in the same week. Aerobic capacity work should be placed on separate days from speed days (not same session).

## SECTION 6: Coach Red Flags

> Patterns that indicate poor conditioning programming. These do not appear in elite programs.

### Session Design Red Flags

**COND-RF-01:** Long conditioning (>20 min at high intensity) before power or speed work.

**COND-RF-02:** Shuttle volume exceeding 800m total in a single session without progression.

**COND-RF-03:** Speed work (max velocity, acceleration) performed under fatigue from prior conditioning.

**COND-RF-04:** High-speed running (>80% max velocity) programmed on recovery or low-load days.

**COND-RF-05:** Conditioning prescribed as punishment or character-building tool.

**COND-RF-06:** Same conditioning protocol used every session (no variation, no periodisation).

**COND-RF-07:** "Go run for 20 minutes" without specifying pace, intensity, or distance target.

**COND-RF-08:** Sprint work programmed after a strength session in the same training block.

**COND-RF-09:** Conditioning volume that exceeds sport practice volume without load management adjustment.

**COND-RF-10:** No differentiation between off-season, pre-season, and in-season conditioning.

**COND-RF-11:** Aerobic capacity training at intensities above Zone 2 (makes it tempo, not base building).

**COND-RF-12:** RSA protocols with rest shorter than 1:3 work:rest (incomplete recovery defeats the purpose).

**COND-RF-13:** Every conditioning session is maximal effort (no periodisation across the week).

### Volume and Intensity Red Flags

**COND-RF-14:** Weekly sprint volume exceeding 600m total for field sport athletes (not including tempo).

**COND-RF-15:** Total weekly conditioning volume exceeding 60 min of high-intensity work (RPE 8+).

**COND-RF-16:** Conditioning session duration exceeding 45 min for field/court sport athletes.

**COND-RF-17:** More than 3 ground contact days per week (plyometric + sprint + conditioning load accumulates).

**COND-RF-18:** High-intensity conditioning on consecutive days.

**COND-RF-19:** All conditioning is linear (no multi-directional work for field/court sport athletes).

**COND-RF-20:** Conditioning volume increases while intensity also increases (only one variable changes at a time).

**COND-RF-21:** MAS protocol using a generic pace (not individualised from MAS test).

### Monitoring and Progression Red Flags

**COND-RF-22:** No heart rate, RPE, or time data collected from conditioning sessions.

**COND-RF-23:** Progression jumps >10% in volume or intensity per week.

**COND-RF-24:** No deload or recovery week integrated into the conditioning block.

**COND-RF-25:** Athlete reports consistent >8 RPE on sessions that should be aerobic capacity (Zone 2).

**COND-RF-26:** Same 30m RSA protocol used for 6+ weeks without progression or variation.

**COND-RF-27:** Conditioning program contains only one energy system (all aerobic or all anaerobic).

**COND-RF-28:** Youth athletes (U16) programmed on the same conditioning protocols as senior athletes.

**COND-RF-29:** Conditioning volume not adjusted for athletes with high match play minutes.

**COND-RF-30:** No consideration of environmental conditions (heat, humidity, altitude) when prescribing conditioning.
