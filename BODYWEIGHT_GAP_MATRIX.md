## 2. DLHD — Double Leg Hip Dominant ⭐ P0

| Diff | Status | Bodyweight Exercises |
|------|--------|---------------------|
| L1 | ~ | Glute Bridge |
| L2 | ✗ | — |
| L3 | ✗ | — |
| L4 | ✗ | — |
| L5 | ✗ | — |

**Blueprint demand:** 24 refs, 11/11/11 across all levels. Highest-demand lower
body family tied with DLKD.

**Gap impact:** Beginner bodyweight DLHD at weeks 3–4 (diff 2–3) requires
cross-family substitution to SLHD on every slot. Intermediate bodyweight DLHD
(diff 2–4) also misses. Advanced bodyweight (diff 3–5) has no SLHD cross-family
option above L3 either.

**Mitigation:** Cross-family to SLHD works for L2–L3 (Split Stance RDL, SL RDL
floor touch, Band-Resisted Nordic) but not L4+.

**Proposal:**

```
Exercise:       Glute Bridge March
Difficulty:     L2
Equipment:      Bodyweight
Family:         DLHD
Progression:    Single-Leg Glute Bridge → Glute Bridge March
Regression:     Glute Bridge
```

**Justification:** Closes the DLHD bodyweight L2 gap. Every intermediate
bodyweight program and beginner week 3–4 currently cross-substitutes DLHD→SLHD.
This restores DLHD's own progression at the most commonly needed level.
Glute Bridge March (alternating leg lifts while holding bridge) is a natural
L1→L2 overload — no equipment, no movement duplication, coach-common.

## 10. Ball — Ballistic ⭐ P0

| Diff | Status | Bodyweight Exercises |
|------|--------|---------------------|
| L1 | ✗ | — |
| L2 | ✗ | — |
| L3 | ✗ | — |
| L4 | ✗ | — |
| L5 | ✗ | — |

**Blueprint demand:** 13 refs, 8/8/8 across levels.

**Gap impact:** Ball has ZERO bodyweight exercises. Every Ball-type exercise
requires a med ball, kettlebell, barbell, or bumpers. This means ALL bodyweight
power programs have empty Ball slots.

**Mandatory blueprints using Ball:** Strength + Power, Power + Speed, Power
Maintenance, Rugby Off-Season. All fail for bodyweight athletes.

**Proposal:**

```
Exercise:       Burpee
Difficulty:     L2
Equipment:      Bodyweight
Family:         Ball
Progression:    Burpee + Jump (tuck jump at top)
Regression:     Step-Back Burpee (no jump)

Exercise:       Burpee + Tuck Jump
Difficulty:     L4
Equipment:      Bodyweight
Family:         Ball
Progression:    Burpee (L2) → Burpee + Tuck Jump
Regression:     Burpee
```

**Justification:** Closes Ball bodyweight gap. Burpee is the single most
coach-common bodyweight ballistic exercise — it involves a drop, push-up, and
explosive jump. It fills the Ball role (whole-body power output, not primarily
plyometric/absorption). L2 covers beginner/intermediate; L4 covers advanced.
No other single exercise covers this gap with equivalent coach adoption.
