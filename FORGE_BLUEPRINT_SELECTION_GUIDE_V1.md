# FORGE Blueprint Selection Guide V1

> Selection criteria for all 14 blueprints.
> Use this guide to match athlete → blueprint for any training phase.
> Evidence base: 58-program corpus across 7 sports.

---

## Blueprint 1: Full Body Strength

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Primary purpose** | General strength accrual across all major movement patterns. Highest per-pattern frequency. |
| **Best athlete type** | Beginner-intermediate, off-season general population, youth (14-17), sport-generalists, multi-sport athletes |
| **Best training age** | 0-3 years of structured strength training |
| **Best season phase** | Off-season, early pre-season, GPP blocks, foundation phase |
| **Best frequency** | 3-4 sessions / week |
| **Contraindications** | Advanced athletes needing specific peak; late pre-season (<4 weeks to competition); in-season maintenance phase; athletes needing hypertrophy-only emphasis; low recovery capacity |
| **Typical outcomes** | Consistent strength gains across squat, hinge, press, pull patterns. Improved movement competency. Foundation for advanced training. Works best as a 4-8 week block before progressing to more specialised work. |
| **Progression path** | → **Strength + Power** (when athlete plateaus on linear progression or needs explosive emphasis) → **Upper / Lower Split** (when per-session volume needs to increase) |
| **Regression path** | ← **Youth Foundation** (if movement competency insufficient) ← **Return to Sport** (if returning from injury) ← **Mixed Modal (GPP)** (as broader foundation) |
| **Example athletes** | EPL Academy U18 player in off-season; BCCI developmental cricketer (year 1-2); NSCA program design athlete; general population S&C beginner |

## Blueprint 2: Strength + Power

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Primary purpose** | Hybrid strength-power development. Heavy compound lifts + explosive work in the same session. |
| **Best athlete type** | Intermediate-advanced, field sport off-season, track & field, rugby pre-season, contact sport athletes |
| **Best training age** | 2-5 years of structured training |
| **Best season phase** | Off-season (primary), pre-season (early blocks), late GPP |
| **Best frequency** | 4-5 sessions / week |
| **Contraindications** | Poor movement quality (fix before loading); early post-injury (<4 weeks cleared); hypertrophy-deficient athletes needing mass first; training age <1 year; in-season maintenance phase |
| **Typical outcomes** | Simultaneous strength and power gains without interference effect. Improved RFD alongside maximal strength. Most commonly used blueprint for serious off-season blocks. |
| **Progression path** | → **Power + Speed** (when power is the limiting factor) → **Sprint Development** (for speed-specialist athletes) → **Upper / Lower Split** (for regional volume emphasis) |
| **Regression path** | ← **Full Body Strength** (when neural fatigue accumulates or technique breaks down) ← **Mixed Modal (GPP)** (when athlete needs broader foundation) |
| **Example athletes** | NZ Rugby off-season strength block athlete; Mike Boyle 4-day strength & power athlete; BCCI-ASCA pre-season cricketer; rugby forward in strength phase |

## Blueprint 3: Strength + Conditioning

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Primary purpose** | Strength maintenance while developing energy system capacity. Pre-season GPP bridging block. |
| **Best athlete type** | Team sport pre-season, returning from off-season break, athletes needing work capacity |
| **Best training age** | 1-4 years of training |
| **Best season phase** | Pre-season (primary), early off-season (return from break), GPP blocks |
| **Best frequency** | 3-4 sessions / week |
| **Contraindications** | Peak strength phase (conditioning interferes with strength adaptation); low recovery capacity; advanced athletes in specific peaking blocks; in-season maintenance (use Power Maintenance instead); 2-a-day training schedules |
| **Typical outcomes** | Improved work capacity and conditioning base. Strength maintained (not maximised). Best used as a 4-6 week bridge between off-season and pre-season. Higher conditioning tolerance over time. |
| **Progression path** | → **Full Body Strength** (when conditioning base is adequate) → **Strength + Power** (when strength needs to be re-prioritised) |
| **Regression path** | ← **Mixed Modal (GPP)** (when broader foundation needed) ← **Youth Foundation** (for younger athletes needing movement base) |
| **Example athletes** | Rugby player returning from off-season break; EPL academy pre-season athlete; BokSmart senior adult rugby player in pre-season; field sport athlete rebuilding after layoff |

## Blueprint 4: Power + Speed

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Primary purpose** | Maximise rate of force development and velocity. Low volume, high intensity, full recovery. |
| **Best athlete type** | Advanced field sport, track sprinters, court sport in-season, peaking phase athletes |
| **Best training age** | 3+ years of structured training (requires established strength base) |
| **Best season phase** | Pre-season peaking, late off-season (last 4 weeks before competition), in-season power maintenance block |
| **Best frequency** | 3-4 sessions / week |
| **Contraindications** | Strength-deficient athletes (<1.5x BW squat M / <1.2x BW squat F — get stronger first); early post-injury; hypertrophy phase; athletes needing general strength foundation; pre-meet taper week |
| **Typical outcomes** | Improved RFD, sprint speed, jump height, reactive strength. Best for peaking power qualities. Short-term (4-6 week) block — not sustainable long-term. |
| **Progression path** | → **Sprint Development** (for dedicated speed focus) → **Power Maintenance** (to retain gains in-season) |
| **Regression path** | ← **Strength + Power** (when strength base is insufficient for power emphasis) ← **Full Body Strength** (when general strength needs attention) |
| **Example athletes** | Track sprinter pre-competition phase; Brazilian National Team sprint program athlete; field sport athlete in speed block; tennis player pre-Grand Slam |

## Blueprint 5: Upper / Lower Split

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Primary purpose** | Higher per-session volume per body region. Two lower sessions, two upper sessions per microcycle. |
| **Best athlete type** | Intermediate-advanced bodybuilding, strength athletes, athletes tolerating high per-session volume well, hypertrophy-focused |
| **Best training age** | 2-5 years of training (requires ability to handle higher per-session volume) |
| **Best season phase** | Off-season hypertrophy blocks, mass accrual phases, strength plateau breakers |
| **Best frequency** | 4 sessions / week (Upper → Lower → Upper → Lower) |
| **Contraindications** | Youth/low training age needing full body frequency (3x/week minimum per pattern); in-season with limited session availability; field sport athletes in competition phase; very long season with high practice load |
| **Typical outcomes** | Higher regional volume per session. Good for hypertrophy and strength plateaus. Each body part trained 2x/week with higher per-session volume than full body approaches. |
| **Progression path** | → **Hypertrophy / Mass Accrual** (when maximal mass gain is the goal) → **Strength + Power** (when power needs emphasis alongside strength) |
| **Regression path** | ← **Full Body Strength** (when recovery management needed or frequency per pattern insufficient) ← **Mixed Modal (GPP)** (as broader foundation) |
| **Example athletes** | Jack Tyler Plan 1/4 rugby in-season athlete; Mike Boyle 4-day split athlete; bodybuilding-phase strength athlete; StrengthLog intermediate split athlete |

## Blueprint 6: Power Maintenance

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Primary purpose** | Retain power qualities with minimal fatigue during season. Low dose, high stimulus, full recovery. |
| **Best athlete type** | In-season team sport, competing court sport, mid-competition phase, tournament blocks |
| **Best training age** | 2+ years (requires established power base to maintain) |
| **Best season phase** | In-season (primary), competition phase, tournament blocks, late-season maintenance |
| **Best frequency** | 1-2 sessions / week |
| **Contraindications** | Off-season (use development blueprints instead — this is too low volume for adaptation); deload/recovery weeks; early return from injury; athletes needing strength development (not yet established); pre-season (too early for maintenance) |
| **Typical outcomes** | Power retained with minimal CNS fatigue. Competitive readiness maintained without compromising recovery. Not a development blueprint — a preservation blueprint. |
| **Progression path** | → **Strength + Power** (when returning to off-season development) → **Power + Speed** (for pre-season peaking) |
| **Regression path** | ← **Deload / Recovery** (if fatigue is too high to maintain even minimal power work) |
| **Example athletes** | In-season EPL soccer player; World Rugby in-season athlete; BCCI cricketer during international series; tennis player during Grand Slam tournament block |

## Blueprint 7: Youth Foundation (U14-U20)

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Primary purpose** | Build movement literacy, general strength, and athleticism. No specialisation — fun + competence + variety. |
| **Best athlete type** | Adolescents (13-20), beginner training age, multi-sport athletes, late-maturing athletes |
| **Best training age** | 0-2 years of structured training |
| **Best season phase** | All phases (year-round base development). Primary off-season and pre-season. In-season: 2x/week maintenance. |
| **Best frequency** | 2-3 sessions / week |
| **Contraindications** | Athletes 20+ (move to adult blueprint); sport-specialised seniors in off-season need more specific loading; post-rehab athletes needing targeted protocol; elite-level athletes in peaking phase |
| **Typical outcomes** | Improved movement competency in squat, hinge, lunge, push, pull. General strength foundation. Athletic skill development. Reduced injury risk in sport practice. |
| **Progression path** | → **Full Body Strength** (when movement competency is established and athlete is ready for loaded work) → **Mixed Modal (GPP)** (for broader athletic foundation) |
| **Regression path** | ← Individual exercise regressions within blueprint (no lower-level blueprint — this is the foundation) |
| **Example athletes** | EPL Academy U14-U16 player; BokSmart U16 youth rugby athlete; USTA developmental tennis junior; multi-sport adolescent athlete |

## Blueprint 8: Court Sport Athletic Development

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Primary purpose** | Multi-directional power, deceleration, change of direction, and lateral strength specific to court sport demands. |
| **Best athlete type** | Tennis, badminton, basketball, volleyball, netball — all phases; cricket fielding specialists |
| **Best training age** | 1-5 years of training |
| **Best season phase** | All phases (volume adjusted by phase: 3-4x off-season, 2-3x in-season, 1-2x tournament) |
| **Best frequency** | 3-4 sessions / week |
| **Contraindications** | Linear-only sport athletes (track, cycling, swimming — use Sprint Development or Strength + Power instead); early rehab phase (use Return to Sport); athletes without established bilateral strength base (need bilateral foundation first) |
| **Typical outcomes** | Improved COD speed, single-leg strength, rotational power, deceleration ability. Court-specific athletic qualities developed beyond what general strength programming provides. |
| **Progression path** | → **Strength + Power** (when bilateral strength needs emphasis alongside court qualities) → **Power + Speed** (for peaking power before competition) |
| **Regression path** | ← **Full Body Strength** (when bilateral strength base is insufficient) ← **Return to Sport** (if injury necessitates rebuilding) |
| **Example athletes** | National Badminton Team athlete; IMG Academy tennis player; Kovacs Institute L1 tennis athlete; BCCI fielding specialist; basketball player in pre-season |

## Blueprint 9: Rugby Off-Season

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Primary purpose** | Build collision-ready strength, power, and mass. Contact preparation with conditioning. |
| **Best athlete type** | Rugby union/league, American football, any contact sport — off-season phase; forwards especially |
| **Best training age** | 2-5 years of training (contact sport requires established technique before contact prep) |
| **Best season phase** | Off-season ONLY (weeks 5-16 of off-season; not first 4 weeks of recovery; not pre-season or in-season) |
| **Best frequency** | 4-5 sessions / week |
| **Contraindications** | In-season (use Power Maintenance instead); non-contact sport; early off-season recovery phase (first 2-4 weeks off — let them recover); athletes with training age <2 years; pre-season (too late for this volume) |
| **Typical outcomes** | Significant strength gains (squat, deadlift, press). Collision readiness. Mass accrual. Contact confidence. Most demanding blueprint in the catalog — best results come from consistent 8-12 week blocks. |
| **Progression path** | → **Strength + Power** (pre-season transition — maintain gains, add speed element) → **Power Maintenance** (for in-season retention) |
| **Regression path** | ← **Full Body Strength** (when training age insufficient for contact prep demands) ← **Mixed Modal (GPP)** (when broader foundation needed) |
| **Example athletes** | NZ Rugby off-season athlete; All Blacks strength block; BokSmart senior adult rugby player; American football off-season lineman |

## Blueprint 10: Sprint Development

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Primary purpose** | Max velocity and/or acceleration improvement. Strength underpins but speed is the priority. |
| **Best athlete type** | Track sprinters (100-400m), field sport speed block, returning from speed loss, acceleration-deficient athletes |
| **Best training age** | 2-5 years (sprint technique requires baseline coordination and strength) |
| **Best season phase** | Pre-season speed block, off-season speed development, early in-season maintenance, dedicated speed mesocycle |
| **Best frequency** | 3-4 sessions / week |
| **Contraindications** | Injury return (Achilles, hamstring, groin — use Return to Sport first); technique-deficient (clean up mechanics at lower intensity first); pre-meet taper week; athletes needing strength foundation first (<1.5x BW squat) |
| **Typical outcomes** | Improved acceleration (0-20m), max velocity (20-60m), sprint mechanics. Strength maintenance. Reactive strength improvement. |
| **Progression path** | → **Power + Speed** (for further power refinement) → **Power Maintenance** (to retain sprint qualities in-season) |
| **Regression path** | ← **Full Body Strength** (when strength base is insufficient for sprint-specific loading) ← **Return to Sport** (if hamstring/Achilles issue) |
| **Example athletes** | StrengthLog off-season sprinter; Brazilian National Team sprint athlete; field sport athlete in dedicated speed block; cricket fast bowler in speed development phase |

## Blueprint 11: Hypertrophy / Mass Accrual

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Primary purpose** | Maximise muscle cross-sectional area. Higher volume, moderate loads, shorter rest, metabolic stress. |
| **Best athlete type** | Bodybuilding phase, underweight athlete, post-rehab mass return, off-season mass block, young athletes needing weight gain |
| **Best training age** | 1-5 years of training (novices can use lower volume approaches first) |
| **Best season phase** | Off-season (primary), post-rehab mass return, GPP blocks, early off-season (first 4-8 weeks) |
| **Best frequency** | 4-6 sessions / week (PPL or Upper-Lower split) |
| **Contraindications** | In-season (unless severely underweight); pre-competition (<6 weeks out); speed/power peaking phases; athletes needing CNS freshness for competition; very long season with high practice volume |
| **Typical outcomes** | Significant muscle mass gain (best in catalog — 9.7/10 credibility score). Improved work capacity and metabolic conditioning. Strength gains secondary to hypertrophy. |
| **Progression path** | → **Strength + Power** (when mass base is adequate and power development becomes priority) → **Upper / Lower Split** (to maintain mass while adding strength emphasis) |
| **Regression path** | ← **Full Body Strength** (when athlete can't recover from high volume) ← **Mixed Modal (GPP)** (as pre-hypertrophy foundation) |
| **Example athletes** | Underweight rugby forward in off-season; post-rehab athlete needing mass return; StrengthLog off-season mass block; NSCA hypertrophy-phase athlete |

## Blueprint 12: Return to Sport (Foundation)

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Primary purpose** | Rebuild movement capacity post-injury. Low intensity, high control, no pain. Bridge between rehab discharge and full training. |
| **Best athlete type** | Post-rehab (cleared by medical/PT), ACL return, hamstring return, shoulder return, general injury return to training |
| **Best training age** | Any (training age is injury-modified — current capacity determines starting point, not history) |
| **Best season phase** | Return-to-play phase only. Bridge between medical discharge and full training integration. |
| **Best frequency** | 3-4 sessions / week |
| **Contraindications** | Unreleased by medical team; active pain during exercise; acute injury phase (<6 weeks post-injury for most soft tissue); psychological unreadiness (fear of re-injury, hesitation); unresolved movement compensations |
| **Typical outcomes** | Safe return to sport-specific loading. Movement confidence restored. Pain-free strength development. Injury-specific reconditioning. Foundation for return to full training in 4-8 weeks. |
| **Progression path** | → **Full Body Strength** (for general return to training) → **Court Sport AD** or **Sprint Development** (sport-dependent progression) → **Strength + Power** (for full training return) |
| **Regression path** | ← Continue with medical team protocol (not a blueprint regression — this is a clinical decision) |
| **Example athletes** | Post-ACL rugby athlete in return-to-sport phase; post-hamstring tear sprinter; post-shoulder labrum tennis player; post-concussion field sport athlete |

## Blueprint 13: Deload / Active Recovery

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Primary purpose** | Systemic recovery, movement practice, connective tissue health. Intent is not stimulus — it's maintenance and regeneration. |
| **Best athlete type** | Any athlete at end of training block, post-competition, high fatigue state, returning from illness, between mesocycles |
| **Best training age** | Any — all athletes need deload periods regardless of training age |
| **Best season phase** | End of mesocycle (weeks 4-6 of any block); post-competition (1-2 weeks); transition between off-season and pre-season; illness return |
| **Best frequency** | 2-3 sessions / week |
| **Contraindications** | Athlete needing stimulus (deloading beyond 14 days causes detraining); pre-competition taper week (use specific taper protocol instead of generic deload); athlete who hasn't completed a full training block (they haven't earned the deload); early off-season (first 2 weeks of off-season ARE the deload) |
| **Typical outcomes** | Reduced systemic fatigue. Movement quality maintained. Connective tissue health improved. Psychological freshness restored. Strength losses minimal (<5% over 7 days). |
| **Progression path** | → Any appropriate development blueprint (Full Body Strength, Strength + Power, Court Sport AD, etc. — sport and phase dependent) |
| **Regression path** | ← N/A — this IS the regression. If athlete needs less than this, they need full rest (no training). |
| **Example athletes** | End of block athlete in any program; post-tournament tennis player; post-season rugby athlete; illness return athlete; athlete between mesocycles |

## Blueprint 14: Mixed Modal (GPP)

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Primary purpose** | Broad athletic capacity across all domains. Nothing maximised, everything developed. Strength, power, speed, conditioning all touched weekly. |
| **Best athlete type** | Off-season multi-sport, beginner athlete, military/tactical, general fitness, pre-specialisation athlete |
| **Best training age** | 0-3 years of training (before specialisation) |
| **Best season phase** | Off-season (primary), pre-specialisation phase, GPP blocks, early off-season general preparation |
| **Best frequency** | 4-5 sessions / week (3 rotating emphasis days: Strength, Power, Conditioning) |
| **Contraindications** | Sport-specialised athlete in-season (needs focused blueprint); advanced athlete needing peaking (needs specificity); any block requiring sport-specific emphasis; late pre-season (<6 weeks to competition); post-rehab athlete (needs controlled loading, not rotation) |
| **Typical outcomes** | Broad athletic foundation. Improved capacity across all 15 movement families. General work capacity and fitness. Identification of strength and weakness areas. Best as a 4-8 week foundation block before specialisation. |
| **Progression path** | → Any sport-specific blueprint: **Full Body Strength** (general sport), **Court Sport AD** (court sports), **Sprint Development** (speed sports), **Rugby Off-Season** (contact sports), **Hypertrophy / Mass Accrual** (mass needs) |
| **Regression path** | ← **Youth Foundation** (for younger/less experienced athletes) ← **Deload / Recovery** (if fatigue is too high for GPP rotation) |
| **Example athletes** | Multi-sport off-season athlete; military/tactical athlete in GPP block; NSCA GPP block athlete; beginner general S&C athlete; off-season athlete before sport specialisation |

## Selection Decision Tree

```
Is the athlete cleared from injury?
├── No  → Return to Sport (12) or continue medical protocol
└── Yes → What is the season phase?

Phase: Off-season (8-16 weeks out)
├── Training age < 2 years → Youth Foundation (7) or Mixed Modal (14)
├── General strength focus → Full Body Strength (1)
├── Mass needed → Hypertrophy / Mass (11)
├── Contact sport prep → Rugby Off-Season (9)
├── Court sport prep → Court Sport AD (8)
└── Speed/power focus → Strength + Power (2)

Phase: Pre-season (2-8 weeks out)
├── Need conditioning base → Strength + Conditioning (3)
├── Need power peaking → Power + Speed (4)
├── Need strength maintenance → Full Body Strength (1)
└── Need speed work → Sprint Development (10)

Phase: In-season (competition)
├── Need power retention → Power Maintenance (6)
├── Need strength maintenance → Full Body Strength (1) at 2x/week
├── Between block break → Deload / Recovery (13)
└── Youth athlete → Youth Foundation (7) at 2x/week

Phase: Between blocks / transition
├── High fatigue → Deload / Recovery (13)
└── Ready for next block → Select by phase above
```
