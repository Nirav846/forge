# FORGE Blueprint Database

## Session Architecture Blueprints from 58+ Elite S&C Programs

---

## Introduction

A blueprint is a reusable session architecture template reverse-engineered from real-world elite programs. Blueprints capture the structural decisions elite coaches make repeatedly: the order in which movement families appear, the relationships between adjacent slots, and the principles that govern exercise selection within each slot. A blueprint is not an exercise list -- it encodes the invariant architectural patterns that persist regardless of which specific exercises a coach chooses. This database catalogs 15 distinct blueprints extracted from 58+ programs spanning cricket, rugby, soccer, athletics, tennis, badminton, and general S&C, with frequency data indicating how commonly each pattern appears across the full sample.

---

## How to Read Blueprints

Each blueprint entry contains the following fields:

- **Blueprint ID** -- Unique identifier (B-01 through B-15).
- **Name** -- Descriptive title.
- **Slot Order** -- Numbered sequence of movement families as they appear in the session.
- **Frequency** -- Percentage of the 58-program sample in which this architecture appears.
- **Primary Sports** -- Sports in which this blueprint most commonly occurs.
- **Season Phase** -- When this blueprint is typically applied.
- **Key Coaching Principle** -- The governing rule that defines this architecture.
- **Example Programs** -- Named programs from which this blueprint was extracted.
- **When to Use** -- Practical guidance for selecting this pattern.

## Blueprint Entries

### B-01: Power-First Gym Strength

**Slot Order**
1. Mobility
2. Activation
3. Power (Olympic Lift / Jump)
4. Bilateral Knee Dominant
5. Bilateral Hip Dominant
6. Horizontal Pull
7. Horizontal Push
8. Core

**Frequency:** 67% of all programs analyzed.

**Primary Sports:** Cricket, Rugby, Soccer, General S&C.

**Season Phase:** All phases (off-season, pre-season, in-season).

**Key Coaching Principle:** Power is always first. High neural-demand exercises must be isolated -- never paired with other demanding movements.

**Example Programs:** BCCI-ASCA Cricket-Specific S&C, DS Performance Cricket 12-Week Pre-Season, EPL Academy Gym Strength, MLS Soccer S&C, NSCA Program Design.

**When to Use:** Default gym strength session for field sport athletes. Use when programming a stand-alone gym session that prioritizes power development alongside strength. Appropriate for all phases; modify volume and intensity per season phase.

### B-02: Speed-Primed Field Session

**Slot Order**
1. Dynamic Warm-Up
2. Sprint Mechanics
3. Linear Speed (Acceleration, 10-20m)
4. Linear Speed (Max Velocity, 60m+)
5. Change of Direction
6. Sport-Specific Integration

**Frequency:** 55% overall; 90% of all field-based speed sessions.

**Primary Sports:** Rugby, Soccer, Athletics, Cricket.

**Season Phase:** Pre-season, off-season, early in-season.

**Key Coaching Principle:** Speed is trained fresh, before any fatigue-inducing work. The progression follows the speed continuum: mechanics first, then acceleration, then max velocity, then COD.

**Example Programs:** Brazilian National Team Sprint Program, Linear Sprint Training Protocol (Nature study), EPL Academy Gamespeed Program, Cricket Fielding-Specific S&C.

**When to Use:** Any session where speed development is the primary objective. Schedule early in the training day or before gym work. Do not place after conditioning or strength work.

### B-03: In-Season Maintenance

**Slot Order**
1. Mobility
2. Activation
3. Power (Light/Moderate)
4. Bilateral Strength (Maintenance)
5. Unilateral Strength
6. Horizontal Pull
7. Core

**Frequency:** 60% of all programs analyzed.

**Primary Sports:** Rugby, Soccer, Cricket.

**Season Phase:** In-season (competition phase).

**Key Coaching Principle:** Volume is reduced but intensity is maintained. Full body approach is preferred over upper/lower splits. The goal is retention, not development.

**Example Programs:** In-Season Rugby Strength & Conditioning (Lift Big Eat Big), World Rugby Sample Strength Session, Jack Tyler Plan 2 and Plan 3 (Full-body variants).

**When to Use:** During competition season when match demands are high and recovery windows are short. Limit to 2 gym sessions per week. Use cluster sets (e.g., 2+2+2 with 30s rest) and back-off sets to maintain quality while minimizing fatigue.

### B-04: Unilateral-Dominant Court Sport

**Slot Order**
1. Mobility
2. Activation
3. Power (Explosive)
4. Unilateral Knee Dominant
5. Unilateral Hip Dominant
6. Change of Direction
7. Horizontal Pull
8. Core

**Frequency:** 45% of all programs analyzed.

**Primary Sports:** Tennis, Badminton, Cricket (fielding).

**Season Phase:** All phases.

**Key Coaching Principle:** Court sports prioritize unilateral work due to sport-specific movement patterns -- lunges, directional changes, and single-leg landings dominate court sport demands.

**Example Programs:** National Badminton Team S&C, Badminton Complex Training Program (PMC study), Cricket Fielding-Specific S&C.

**When to Use:** Primary gym template for court sport athletes. Unilateral knee work (lunges, step-ups) and unilateral hip work (single-leg RDL, split squats) precede bilateral work. COD work may be included as a strength supplement after the primary lifts.

### B-05: Complex Training (Strength + Plyometric Alternation)

**Slot Order**
1. Mobility
2. Activation
3. Power / Plyometric
4. Strength Exercise
5. Power / Plyometric
6. Strength Exercise
7. Core

**Frequency:** 35% of all programs analyzed.

**Primary Sports:** Badminton, Athletics, Rugby.

**Season Phase:** Pre-season, off-season (power development blocks).

**Key Coaching Principle:** Alternating strength and plyometric exercises in the same session potentiates neural drive. The strength exercise enhances the subsequent plyometric through post-activation potentiation (PAP).

**Example Programs:** Badminton Complex Training Program (8-week intervention), Brazilian National Team Sprint Program (strength day).

**When to Use:** During dedicated power development blocks. Effective for athletes who need simultaneous strength and explosive power improvements. Complex pairs must be biomechanically similar (e.g., squat + jump squat, deadlift + broad jump).

### B-06: Olympic Lift Integration

**Slot Order**
1. Mobility
2. Activation
3. Olympic Lift (Clean / Snatch Variation)
4. Bilateral Knee Dominant
5. Bilateral Hip Dominant
6. Horizontal Pull
7. Vertical Push
8. Core

**Frequency:** 50% of all programs analyzed.

**Primary Sports:** Rugby, General S&C, Soccer.

**Season Phase:** Off-season, pre-season.

**Key Coaching Principle:** Olympic lifts are the preferred power development tool in elite rugby and general S&C programs. They occupy the power slot and are never paired with other demanding exercises.

**Example Programs:** NZ Rugby / All Blacks Strength Foundation, Mike Boyle 4-Day Strength & Power Template (Power Days), In-Season Rugby (Lift Big Eat Big).

**When to Use:** When the coach and athletes have technical proficiency in Olympic lifting. Appropriate for off-season and pre-season blocks. Not recommended for in-season maintenance unless the athlete is already highly proficient. Vertical push replaces horizontal push in this variant due to the overhead nature of the snatch/clean variants.

### B-07: Rotational Power Emphasis

**Slot Order**
1. Mobility
2. Activation
3. Rotational Power (Medicine Ball / Cable)
4. Bilateral Strength
5. Horizontal Pull
6. Anti-Rotational Core

**Frequency:** 40% of all programs analyzed.

**Primary Sports:** Cricket, Tennis, Badminton.

**Season Phase:** All phases.

**Key Coaching Principle:** Rotational power is sport-specific for torque-demand sports. Cricket (batting, bowling), tennis (groundstrokes, serve), and badminton (overhead smashes, rotational recovery) all require high-velocity trunk rotation.

**Example Programs:** Cricket 12-Week Pre-Season (power emphasis), USTA Player Development Training Blocks, ATP Pre-Match Strength Circuit.

**When to Use:** Primary or supplementary blueprint for rotational sport athletes. Medicine ball throws, cable rotations, and anti-rotation work (Pallof press, chops) are the primary tools. Can be layered onto B-01 or B-04 by inserting rotational power after activation.

### B-08: Tri-Set Structure

**Slot Order**
1. Power Slot (Standalone)
2. Tri-Set A: Strength Exercise A + Strength Exercise B + Core/Mobility
3. Tri-Set B: Strength Exercise C + Strength Exercise D + Core/Mobility
4. Accessory Work

**Frequency:** 30% of all programs analyzed.

**Primary Sports:** General S&C (Mike Boyle system).

**Season Phase:** All phases.

**Key Coaching Principle:** Tri-sets allow comprehensive coverage while keeping workouts under 60 minutes. Each tri-set combines two major exercises with a core or mobility movement. The power slot remains standalone.

**Example Programs:** Mike Boyle Functional Training System, Mike Boyle 4-Day Strength & Power Template, Mike Boyle CFSC 3-Day Program.

**When to Use:** When time efficiency is critical. Ideal for high-volume training environments (team S&C) where session duration must be controlled. The tri-set structure maintains training density while respecting cortisol management principles.

### B-09: Training Block Periodization

**Slot Order (Phase Structure)**
- Block 1 (4-6 weeks): Strength Foundation -- moderate reps, bilateral emphasis, progressive overload
- Block 2 (4-6 weeks): Power Development -- Olympic lifts, plyometrics, lower reps, higher velocity
- Block 3 (2 weeks): Taper / Competition Prep -- reduced volume, maintained intensity, sport-specific work

**Frequency:** 40% of all programs analyzed.

**Primary Sports:** Tennis, General S&C, Cricket.

**Season Phase:** Off-season to pre-season transition.

**Key Coaching Principle:** 4-6 weeks is the minimum duration required for meaningful physiological adaptation, particularly strength gain. Blocks are sequenced from general (strength foundation) to specific (power/competition).

**Example Programs:** USTA Player Development Training Blocks, Tennis Grand Slam Mesocycle, Damon Bednarski Cricket 12-Week Pre-Season (3-phase), Cricfit Fast Bowling 12-Week Program (3-phase).

**When to Use:** When designing a structured off-season or pre-season training cycle. Block 3 (taper) is critical for competition readiness. Do not extend blocks beyond 6 weeks without a deload or phase transition.

### B-10: Full Body -- No Splits

**Session Structure**
- Session 1: Full Body (Power + Strength + Pull + Push + Core)
- Session 2: Full Body (Power + Strength + Pull + Push + Core)
- Session 3: Full Body (Power + Strength + Pull + Push + Core)

**Frequency:** 45% of all programs analyzed.

**Primary Sports:** Rugby (in-season), General S&C.

**Season Phase:** In-season (primary), also off-season.

**Key Coaching Principle:** Body-part splits are considered redundant for modern professional athletes. Full body training better manages fatigue, allows higher training frequency per movement pattern, and aligns with the multi-dimensional demands of field and court sports.

**Example Programs:** In-Season Rugby (Lift Big Eat Big), World Rugby Strength Sessions, Jack Tyler Plan 2 and Plan 3, NSCA Flexible Nonlinear Templates.

**When to Use:** Default weekly structure for field sport athletes. Three full-body sessions per week is the most common frequency. Each session covers the same movement families but exercises can vary to introduce variation and manage fatigue.

### B-11: Lower-Upper Split (Jack Tyler Rugby)

**Slot Order (Multiple Variants)**

**Plan 1 (Lower-Upper)**
- Day A (Lower): Mobility -> Power -> Bilateral Lower -> Unilateral Lower -> Core
- Day B (Upper): Mobility -> Horizontal Push -> Horizontal Pull -> Vertical Push -> Vertical Pull -> Core

**Plan 4 (Lower-Upper V2)**
- Day A: Power -> Bilateral Lower -> Horizontal Push -> Core
- Day B: Power -> Unilateral Lower -> Horizontal Pull -> Vertical Pull

**Frequency:** Found in 4 Jack Tyler rugby in-season templates.

**Primary Sports:** Rugby (in-season).

**Season Phase:** In-season.

**Key Coaching Principle:** Lower-upper split allows higher per-session volume for each domain while managing fatigue across the training week. Contrasts with the full-body approach (B-10) by distributing volume across two distinct sessions.

**Example Programs:** Jack Tyler Plan 1 (Lower-Upper-Full), Jack Tyler Plan 4 (Lower-Upper-Full V2).

**When to Use:** Alternative to full-body in-season training when the coach wants greater per-session volume for lower or upper body. Effective for 2-day in-season schedules where each session targets different movement domains. Not recommended for 3-day schedules -- full body is more appropriate at that frequency.

### B-12: StrengthLog 3-Phase Periodization (Sprint / Badminton)

**Phase Structure**

**Phase 1 (Weeks 1-4): Accumulation / Strength Endurance**
- Higher volume (3x12-15 reps)
- Shorter rest (60-90s)
- Moderate intensity (60-70% 1RM)
- Goal: tissue readiness, movement proficiency, endurance base

**Phase 2 (Weeks 5-8): Intensification / Pure Strength**
- Lower volume (3x6-8 reps)
- Longer rest (2-3 min)
- Higher intensity (75-85% 1RM)
- Goal: maximal strength development

**Phase 3 (Weeks 9-12): Pre-Comp / Speed & Power**
- Mixed explosive + heavy work
- Contrast training, complex pairs
- Sport-specific movement integration
- Goal: transfer strength to sport performance

**Frequency:** Found in multiple StrengthLog program templates.

**Primary Sports:** Sprint (track and field), Badminton.

**Season Phase:** Off-season to pre-season progression.

**Key Coaching Principle:** Linear 3-phase progression with 4-week blocks moves from high-volume/low-intensity to low-volume/high-intensity to sport-specific power. Each block builds on the adaptation from the previous block.

**Example Programs:** StrengthLog Off-Season Sprint Program (12-week), StrengthLog Pre-Season Sprint Program (12-week), StrengthLog Badminton Program (12-week).

**When to Use:** Athletes with a 12-week off-season or pre-season window requiring a systematic strength progression. Suitable for sports where the annual calendar allows clear block structure. The 4-week block duration is short enough to maintain momentum but long enough for adaptation.

### B-14: Badminton Insight 4-Phase System

**Phase Structure**

**Phase 1: Strength Endurance (4-6 weeks)**
- 3x12-15 reps per exercise
- 60-90s rest intervals
- Compound movement focus
- Goal: build work capacity, tendon conditioning, movement foundation

**Phase 2: Pure Strength (4-6 weeks)**
- 3x4-6 reps per exercise
- 2-3 min rest intervals
- Heavy compound lifts
- Goal: maximal strength, neural adaptation

**Phase 3: Speed & Power (4-6 weeks)**
- Contrast training methods
- Complex pairs (strength + plyometric)
- Explosive movement focus
- Goal: rate of force development, power output

**Phase 4: Power Endurance / Robustness (4-6 weeks)**
- Circuit-based conditioning
- Sport-specific work-to-rest ratios
- Injury prevention maintenance
- Goal: sustain power output through match duration

**Frequency:** 4 distinct phases documented by Badminton Insight.

**Primary Sports:** Badminton.

**Season Phase:** Year-round periodization (each phase maps to a competitive period).

**Key Coaching Principle:** Periodized across the competitive year with a different focus each mesocycle. The system cycles through strength endurance, pure strength, speed and power, and power endurance to develop complete athletic capacity for badminton's intermittent high-intensity demands.

**Example Programs:** Badminton Insight Strength Endurance Phase, Badminton Insight Pure Strength Phase, Badminton Insight Speed & Power Phase, Badminton Insight Power Endurance Phase.

**When to Use:** Complete year-round system for badminton athletes. Phase selection depends on competitive calendar: Strength Endurance in early off-season, Pure Strength in late off-season, Speed & Power in pre-season, Power Endurance during competition period. Can be truncated to 3 phases (omit Strength Endurance) for athletes with adequate base.

### B-15: IMG Tennis / Kovacs Court Sport

**Slot Order**

**IMG Academy Tennis Session**
1. Dynamic Warm-Up
2. Agility
3. Power
4. Strength
5. Core
6. Conditioning

**Kovacs Institute Adult Tennis L1**
1. Dynamic Warm-Up
2. Linear Speed
3. Lateral Agility
4. Power
5. Strength
6. Elasticity (reactive strength)
7. Core

**Frequency:** IMG Academy Tennis S&C, Kovacs Institute methodology.

**Primary Sports:** Tennis, Court Sports.

**Season Phase:** All phases (periodized within the session framework).

**Key Coaching Principle:** Agility work precedes power in court sport blueprints -- the reverse of the standard field sport sequence. This reflects the primacy of multidirectional court movement over raw power output in tennis and similar sports. Linear speed and lateral agility are treated as separate training stimuli.

**Example Programs:** IMG Academy Tennis S&C (2-3x/week, year-round), Kovacs Institute Adult Tennis L1 (6-week foundations).

**When to Use:** Primary session architecture for tennis and court sport athletes. Use the IMG variant for general court sport S&C. Use the Kovacs variant when greater emphasis on speed mechanics is required (developmental athletes, early pre-season). Elasticity work (plyometrics, reactive jumps) may be added as a separate slot following strength, as in the Kovacs model.

## Usage Guide

### Selecting the Right Blueprint

**By Sport Domain**

- **Field Sports (Rugby, Soccer):** B-01 (Power-First Gym) as default gym session. B-02 (Speed-Primed Field) for field sessions. B-03 (In-Season Maintenance) during competition. B-10 (Full Body) for weekly structure. B-13 (BokSmart) for rugby-specific guidelines.
- **Court Sports (Tennis, Badminton):** B-04 (Unilateral-Dominant) as default gym session. B-07 (Rotational Power) when rotary demands are high. B-14 (Badminton Insight) and B-15 (IMG Tennis/Kovacs) for sport-specific periodization.
- **Track Sports (Athletics/Sprint):** B-02 (Speed-Primed Field) for track sessions. B-12 (StrengthLog 3-Phase) for off-season strength periodization. B-05 (Complex Training) for power development blocks.
- **Cricket:** B-01 (Power-First Gym) as default. B-04 (Unilateral-Dominant) for fielding-specific sessions. B-07 (Rotational Power) for batting/bowling emphasis. B-09 (Training Block Periodization) for pre-season structure.
- **General S&C / Multi-Sport:** B-08 (Tri-Set) for time-efficient sessions. B-06 (Olympic Lift Integration) when coaching Olympic lifting. B-10 (Full Body) for weekly programming. B-09 (Block Periodization) for macro-cycle design.

**By Season Phase**

- **Off-Season (4-12 weeks):** B-09 (Block Periodization), B-12 (StrengthLog 3-Phase), B-14 (Badminton Insight Phases 1-3). Higher volume, lower intensity initially; progress toward power.
- **Pre-Season (2-6 weeks):** B-01, B-02, B-05, B-06. Mixed power and strength. Sport-specific integration increases. Volume begins to decrease.
- **In-Season (competition period):** B-03 (In-Season Maintenance), B-10 (Full Body), B-11 (Lower-Upper Split). Reduced volume, maintained intensity. 2 gym sessions per week standard.
- **Post-Season / Transition (1-3 weeks):** Active recovery. Low-intensity work. No structured blueprint required. Focus on restoration.

**By Athlete Level**

- **Youth / Developmental (U16):** B-13 youth variant (Movement Skills focus). Modify B-01 by omitting power slot. Emphasize technique and bilateral strength foundations.
- **Developmental (U19 / College):** B-01 with lighter power loading. B-10 for full-body frequency. B-02 for speed development. Progressively introduce Olympic lift variations.
- **Elite / Professional:** All blueprints applicable. Selection depends on sport, phase, and individual athlete profile. Elite athletes may layer blueprints across the training week.

### Combining Blueprints

Most elite programs combine multiple blueprints within a single training week. Common combinations:

- **Week Template (Field Sport, In-Season):** B-02 (field session) + B-03 (gym session 1) + B-03 (gym session 2) = 3 sessions total.
- **Week Template (Court Sport, Off-Season):** B-15 (court conditioning) + B-04 (gym session 1) + B-07 (gym session 2) + B-04 (gym session 3) = 4 sessions total.
- **Mesocycle Template (12-Week Pre-Season):** B-12 Phase 1 (Weeks 1-4) -> B-12 Phase 2 (Weeks 5-8) -> B-12 Phase 3 (Weeks 9-12), with B-02 field sessions integrated throughout.
