# FORGE Warmup Library V1

**Purpose:** Structured warmup layer with 100+ drills, progressions,
regressions, and coaching cues. Designed to sit before the main session.

**Source methodologies:** NSCA, ASCA, UKSCA, CFSC (Functional), EXOS,
ALTIS, BCCI, Rugby S&C, Tennis S&C, Badminton S&C.

---

## Part 1 — Universal Warmup Architecture

Every warmup follows a 4-phase RAMP structure:

| Phase | Purpose | Duration | Intensity |
|-------|---------|----------|-----------|
| 1. Raise | Increase HR, blood flow, body temp | 3-5 min | Low (RPE 2-3) |
| 2. Activate & Mobilise | Prime key muscle groups, take joints through ROM | 5-8 min | Low-Moderate (RPE 3-4) |
| 3. Potentiate | Sport/ session-specific prep at near-training intensity | 5-8 min | Moderate-High (RPE 5-7) |
| 4. Prepare | Final rehearsal of session's primary movements | 3-5 min | Training intensity |

Total: 16-26 min. Adjustable by athlete level and session type.

### RAMP by session type

| Session type | Raise | Activate/Mobilise | Potentiate | Prepare |
|-------------|-------|-------------------|------------|---------|
| Strength | 3 min jog/skipping | Joint mobility + band activation | Light compound work (40-50%) | Build to working weight |
| Power | 4 min dynamic | Plyo prep + mobility | Sub-max explosive movements | Sport-specific jumps/throws |
| Conditioning | 5 min light run | Dynamic stretching + mobility | Movement prep at goal pace | Brief bout at target intensity |
| Speed | 5 min progressive run | Hip mobility + drills | Acceleration drills at 70-80% | Build to full speed |
| Competition | 8-12 min sport-specific | Full mobility + activation | Sport movements at intensity | Position-specific rehearsal |

## Part 4 — Coaching Principles

### 4.1 Warmup progression rules

1. **Never static stretch cold.** All static stretching is reserved for
   after activation or post-session.
2. **Low to high intensity.** RAMP phases progress from low (RPE 2) to
   training intensity (RPE 7+).
3. **Joint prep before activation.** Mobility drills precede strength
   activation within the Activate phase.
4. **Sport-specific before explosive.** Potentiation should mirror the
   session's primary movement pattern.
5. **Warmup duration scales with session intensity.** A max-effort speed
   session gets a longer warmup than an aerobic conditioning session.
6. **Youth warmups are shorter and more playful.** 12-16 min max for
   youth. Include games and competition in warmup drills.

### 4.2 RAMP timing by athlete level

| Level | Raise | Activate | Potentiate | Prepare | Total |
|-------|-------|----------|------------|---------|-------|
| Beginner | 3 min | 5 min | 3 min | 3 min | 14 min |
| Intermediate | 4 min | 6 min | 5 min | 4 min | 19 min |
| Advanced | 5 min | 7 min | 7 min | 5 min | 24 min |

### 4.3 Equipment needed

| Drill type | Equipment |
|------------|-----------|
| Raise | None |
| Hip mobility | Mat (optional) |
| Thoracic mobility | PVC pipe, foam roller |
| Shoulder | Band, PVC pipe |
| Activation | Mini bands, glute bands |
| Potentiation | Box, med ball, band |
| Sport-specific | Sport equipment (bat, racket, ball) |
